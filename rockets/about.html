Bleacher Report Breakdown Article Russell Westbrook Playoff Woes: Is he Consistent Team Cohesion Issues: Was the Westbrook the Problem A Blasphemous Proposal: Seasonal Issues Small Ball Test: Why did this even work for as long as it did?
I love watching good angry basketball. Not a bunch of teams shooting or flashy layups, but instead some aggressive dunks and demeaning blocks. For this reason, I always enjoyed watching Russell Westbrook (right) play; he does exactly as advertised: run and dunk.  When I am not refilling Hoover Dam with the tears that come complementary being a Knicks fan, I enjoy rooting for the Houston Rockets. This season of basketball was groundbreaking for me personally, because although the Rockets lost a great point guard in Chris Paul, they managed to snag Russell Westbrook to pair with James Harden (left), creating one of the best offensive backcourts in the league (on paper). Two ringless MVPs with chips on their shoulders, plus coach Mike D’Antoni’s system and the strong supporting cast gave off the illusion of a well rounded championship contending team. Even I thought the conference finals was in sight. But then came the actual season and everything blew up. What started out strong fizzled out pretty quickly. I made this page to manifest the thoughts that have been circling in my head for a while now. I truly think that the Rockets are a piece away from a title, so there is no need to rebuild the team from the ground up. Yeah they are a pretty old team, but the talent is there. I thought it could be fun to act as the general manager of the Rockets and reconstruct the team in a way that I think would win.
